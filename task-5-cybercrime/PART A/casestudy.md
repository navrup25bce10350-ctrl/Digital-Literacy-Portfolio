# UPI Payment Fraud – A Case Study

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common forms of cybercrime in India, targeting individuals who frequently use digital payment applications. This type of fraud usually involves tricking users into approving unauthorized transactions.

A typical scenario begins when the victim receives a phone call, message, or link from a scammer posing as a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer or seller on an online platform. The scammer creates a sense of urgency or trust and sends a “collect request” on a UPI app, claiming that the user needs to approve it to receive money. In reality, approving such a request results in money being debited from the victim’s account.

In some cases, victims are asked to share sensitive information such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or are directed to fake websites that mimic legitimate payment platforms. Once the fraud is successful, the money is quickly transferred to multiple accounts, making recovery difficult.

Students and young adults are common targets due to their frequent use of digital payment apps and lack of awareness. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and compromised personal data.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and caution while performing online transactions, especially when dealing with unknown sources or unexpected requests.
